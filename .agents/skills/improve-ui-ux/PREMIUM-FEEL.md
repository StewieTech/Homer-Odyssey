# Premium Feel

Premium feel is perceived product quality. It overlaps with usability but is not the same thing. A screen can be usable and still feel cheap if its hierarchy, state handling, copy, motion, and spacing are unresolved.

For your product, premium should mean calm, emotionally safe, intelligent, fast, and slightly aspirational. It should not mean flashy.

## The Five Traits

**Calm**
- Clear hierarchy
- Confident spacing
- Low visual noise
- No competing calls to action
- Copy that reduces anxiety

**Fast**
- Immediate acknowledgement of user input
- Useful loading, skeleton, or optimistic states
- No ambiguous waits
- Perceived performance protected during AI/provider latency

**Intentional**
- Every element has a job
- Alignment and spacing look designed, not accidental
- Motion communicates state, continuity, or feedback
- Icons and controls match expected conventions

**Trustworthy**
- Payment, account, privacy, and AI behavior are clear at the moment trust is needed
- No dark-pattern pressure
- Correction tone feels safe
- Errors explain recovery instead of blame

**Delightful**
- Small moments of personality without clutter
- Strong empty and success states
- Gentle motion or haptics where useful
- Microcopy that feels precise and human

## Premium Smells

- Large decorative surfaces that do not help the task
- Generic cards, gradients, or hero layouts that hide the product
- Loading states that feel like the app froze
- Empty states that blame the user or say nothing useful
- Dense controls before the user understands value
- Inconsistent spacing or accidental alignment
- Copy that sounds like internal implementation
- Correction UI that makes mistakes feel public or final

## Review Prompt

Ask:

- What feels calm?
- What feels fast?
- What feels intentional?
- What feels trustworthy?
- What feels delightful?
- What feels generic, noisy, slow, fragile, or emotionally unsafe?

Then turn each answer into a concrete recommendation.
